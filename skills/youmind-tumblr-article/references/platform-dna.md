# Tumblr Platform DNA

Tumblr rewards posts that feel like they belong to a living blog, not a content warehouse.

## Core surfaces

- **Blog identity first**: you publish to a specific blog, not an abstract channel.
- **Dashboard preview first**: the title, first screen, and first paragraph determine whether the user expands.
- **Notes are the social layer**: likes matter less than replies and reblogs with commentary.
- **Tags are discovery hints**: a small number of sharp tags beats a broad SEO cluster.
- **Queue is editorial infrastructure**: Tumblr supports cadence as a first-class workflow, not just instant publishing.

## Format choices

### Text post

Best when:

- the thesis is the main event
- the post needs sections, quotes, and a point of view
- the image is support, not the center

### Photo post

Best when:

- there is one strong image or visual asset
- the caption can carry the whole meaning in a compact form
- the user wants something closer to a visual note than a mini-essay

Bad fit:

- decorative image + long essay
- image carousel logic that really belongs elsewhere

## Voice that travels on Tumblr

Usually rewarded:

- a clear perspective
- a sentence that can be quoted or reblogged
- less corporate varnish
- creator energy over institutional tone

Usually punished:

- generic “this article explores” openings
- hard-sell CTA stacks
- over-polished SEO intros
- long abstraction before anything concrete happens

## Image strategy

Good Tumblr image use:

- a single lead image with a clean caption
- screenshots with interpretation
- visual artifacts with creator commentary

Weak image use:

- stock-photo filler
- image unrelated to the actual point
- image-heavy markup that turns into layout noise

## Feedback model

If the user asks about “comments”, translate that into Tumblr-native signals:

- **notes / conversation mode** for replies and reblogs with text
- **notifications** for broader activity around the blog
- **followers** for audience snapshot

Do not flatten Tumblr into a generic comment-count platform.

## Operational constraints

- Keep HTML simple
- Avoid scripts and fragile embeds
- Be prepared for post URLs to resolve differently than dashboard URLs
- Hostname can vary across standard subdomains and custom domains, so connection-time blog identity matters
