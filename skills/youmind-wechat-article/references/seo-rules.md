# WeChat SEO & Distribution Heuristics

> This file is intentionally conservative.
> Exact recommendation weights and traffic-pool thresholds are not public platform truth, so the skill must not write as if it has internal algorithm numbers.

---

## Evidence Boundary

Use three layers:

- **Public platform truth:** what official docs or clearly confirmed platform behavior support
- **Research-backed heuristic:** what WeChat studies and digital-reading research support
- **Operational default:** what this skill chooses as a practical default

If a claim belongs to layer 2 or 3, label it that way in reasoning. Do not present it as official.

## What Likely Matters for Distribution

Without pretending to know internal weights, professional practice should optimize for:

- willingness to open
- title/body consistency
- originality and account trust
- first-screen retention
- read depth and structural clarity
- social actions such as save, comment, 在看, and share
- a clear next action after reading

Think in systems:

`title -> first screen -> delivery quality -> social value -> next action`

## Title Optimization

Research and case-study consensus both suggest the title has outsized influence on whether the article gets a serious chance.

### Operational defaults

- In this skill, keep the working title concise and mobile-first
- Put the subject, audience, or payoff in the first half
- Front-load what the reader gets; do not bury it behind flourish
- Use specificity before rhetoric

### 5 Title Strategies

**1. Number + Specificity:**
> "5年产品经理总结的3个反直觉认知" / "我面试了200个人之后发现的真相"
Best when the number carries real structure or proof, not decoration.

**2. Information Gap:**
> "90%的人不知道的简历潜规则" / "被忽略的GPT用法，比写代码更值钱"
Use only when the body clearly pays off the gap.

**3. Counterintuitive:**
> "别再学Python了" / "真正决定收入的不是能力，是这件小事"
High risk, high reward. Must cash out with real reasoning, not attitude.

**4. Pain-Point Direct:**
> "为什么你的PPT总是被打回" / "月薪5000和50000的人，差距在周末"
Names the problem the reader is experiencing.

**5. Identity Trigger:**
> "30岁还在纠结要不要考研的人" / "给所有被领导PUA过的打工人"
Reader self-identifies and feels personally addressed.

### Title Craft Details

**What titles should usually achieve:**

1. Reader fit: "this is about me / my scenario"
2. Concrete payoff: "I gain something specific"
3. One tension: "there is an angle or contradiction worth entering"

**Always generate 3 title alternatives** with different strategies. Score each on:
1. Specificity (does it promise something concrete?)
2. Curiosity gap (does it make you NEED to know?)
3. Self-identification (does the target reader feel addressed?)
4. Fulfillment risk (can the body really deliver what the title promises?)

### Title Anti-Patterns
- Clickbait that weakens title/body alignment
- Generic titles without specificity
- Pseudo-official or panic-inducing phrasing
- Punctuation tricks doing the real work of the title
- Clever rhetorical questions with weak relevance

## Digest (摘要) Optimization

- Hard limit: stay within WeChat byte constraints
- Must complement the title, not repeat it
- Best when it adds one more concrete reason to click
- Prefer a specific payoff, scene, or unfinished implication over generic summary

Example:
> Title: "5年产品经理总结的3个反直觉认知"
> Digest: "第二个我当年不信，结果踩了半年的坑才服气。" (24 chars, specific, creates curiosity)

## In-Article Relevance and Searchability

### Keyword Placement Strategy

| Position | Rule |
|----------|------|
| First 200 characters | Core keyword must appear at least once |
| Full article | Repeat naturally enough for thematic consistency |
| H2 subheadings | Use long-tail variations when they genuinely fit |
| Image alt text | Include keywords only where relevant |

### Keyword Density Rules
- Natural integration only
- Do not stuff
- Prefer one stable core term plus a small number of related long-tail variants
- Search intent fit matters more than raw repetition

### Keyword Research Methods
1. **WeChat Index** (微信指数) — volume data for trending terms
2. **WeChat search autocomplete** — type partial queries, note suggestions (= long-tail gold)
3. **Competitor analysis** — what keywords do high-performing articles in your vertical use?
4. **Reader comments/DMs** — the exact words your audience uses to describe their problems
5. **5118 / Aizhan** — cross-platform keyword research for broader context

## Search and Recommendation Heuristics

Publicly, exact weights are unknown. Operationally, behave as if WeChat rewards:

- account consistency and topic clarity
- originality and trust
- user satisfaction signals
- title relevance
- readable structure
- meaningful interaction

### Tag Strategy

- use tags to improve semantic clarity, not to stuff buzzwords
- specific tags beat broad labels
- tags should align with title, body, and actual article promise
- if tags feel like metadata noise, remove them

## Completion Rate Optimization

Do not anchor on fake thresholds. Optimize for readable depth instead.

### Structural Techniques

| Technique | Impact |
|-----------|--------|
| Line break every 2-4 short lines | Creates mobile breathing room |
| Bold key information | Creates anchor points for scanning |
| Section subheadings every few screens | Helps re-entry after distraction |
| Mid-article question or turn | Resets attention |
| Visual or structural shift every 2-4 screens | Breaks monotony |
| Never let the article feel like one long slab | Reduces abandonment |

### Content Techniques
- Front-load value in the first screen
- Make each paragraph do one job
- Give readers a forwarding reason, not only a reading reason
- Vary paragraph length and cadence
- Use concrete proof before abstract conclusion

---

## End-of-Article Optimization

Endings should be designed around the article's `primary_conversion_goal`.

### Footer Design Checklist
1. Name one next step
2. Make the step low-friction
3. Match the CTA to the article promise
4. Add internal continuation when relevant
5. Never use coercive share language

---

## Gotchas

**"Pseudo-precision":** If you cannot source the number, do not write exact weight, threshold, or pool size claims.

**"Keyword stuffing reflex":** Over-repetition signals low quality to both readers and ranking systems.

**"Search-only thinking":** Searchability is a multiplier, not a substitute for value delivery.

**"Ignoring share value":** If the article is searchable but not forwardable, it leaves social distribution on the table.
