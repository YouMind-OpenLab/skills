# Attention, Sharing, and CTA Psychology

> Rules card, not a literature review.
> Use it when titles, openings, sharing logic, or CTA quality feel weak.

> This file captures research-backed heuristics for公众号 writing.
> Most studies are not公众号-exclusive; treat them as transferable evidence, not platform law.

---

## Evidence Boundary

There are three evidence tiers in this file:

- **Direct WeChat evidence:** studies or reports specifically observing WeChat article behavior
- **Transferable digital reading evidence:** mobile/news/social reading studies that apply well to公众号 consumption
- **Managerial inference:** operational rules derived from those studies

Never present the inferred layer as if WeChat officially confirmed it.

---

## 1. Mobile Reading Means Early Value Wins

Research on mobile and fragmented reading suggests:

- mobile reading is easier to interrupt
- mind wandering increases scanning and shallower processing
- fragmented reading raises working-memory and switching costs

Operational rule:

- front-load value
- do not hide the payoff after long setup
- ensure each screen can be understood quickly

Practical defaults:

- answer "why read this?" in the first screen
- keep one point per paragraph when possible
- reset rhythm every 2-4 screens

---

## 2. Sharing Is a Social-Value Decision

People do not share only because content is "good."
They share because it helps them do something socially:

- help a specific person
- signal taste or judgment
- show identity alignment
- start a conversation
- express a reaction others will recognize

Operational rule:

Before drafting, define:

- who will forward this
- to whom
- with what one-line forwarding message

If you cannot imagine the forwarding sentence, the article's share value is weak.

---

## 3. Concrete Language Beats Abstract Language

Recent sharing research consistently favors concrete, specific language over abstract language.

Operational rule:

- replace "很重要 / 值得关注 / 影响深远" with named stakes, scenes, numbers, or actions
- give one vivid example before generalization
- prefer "what exactly changes for the reader" over broad significance claims

Title implication:

- specificity is usually stronger than vagueness
- "who + what payoff + where the tension is" beats symbolic grandiosity

---

## 4. Titles Must Fulfill, Not Merely Tease

Transferable and WeChat-specific evidence suggests:

- rhetorical or over-clever titles can underperform if they weaken relevance
- clickbait features are not universally positive
- title/body relevance matters for sharing and trust

Operational rule:

Titles should usually do 3 things:

1. identify the reader or scenario
2. promise a concrete payoff
3. signal one tension or surprising angle

Avoid:

- empty suspense
- exaggerated fear
- pseudo-official wording
- punctuation tricks as the main engine

---

## 5. Emotion Helps, but Precision Matters More

Emotion can improve sharing, but "more negative" is not the rule.
Different discrete emotions behave differently, and over-intensity can create resistance.

Operational rule:

- use high-arousal emotion only when it is supported by evidence or real scene detail
- prefer earned surprise, recognition, relief, embarrassment, or sharp clarity over pure outrage bait
- emotional language must still map to something verifiable

---

## 6. Narrative Works Best with Utility

Narrative transportation is real, but story is not an automatic upgrade.
The story must help readers interpret meaning, not just decorate the article.

Operational rule:

- if using story, connect it quickly to the reader's decision or tension
- do not spend too much of the first screen on scene-setting with no value
- for practical articles, alternate story with conclusion and explanation

Good pattern:

`scene -> interpretation -> implication -> action`

---

## 7. CTA Works When It Removes Friction

Evidence on messaging suggests CTA performance comes less from cosmetic wording and more from removing real barriers.

Operational rule:

CTA should answer:

- what exactly to do next
- why it is worth doing
- what friction is removed

Examples:

- bad: `点击了解更多`
- better: `3 分钟看完完整清单`
- better: `回复关键词领取可直接复用模板`
- better: `如果你正准备搭这个流程，下一篇就是具体表格和模板`

---

## 8. Unsupported Myths to Avoid

- more punctuation does not reliably mean higher long-term performance
- stronger negativity does not reliably mean stronger sharing
- more images does not automatically improve spread
- story is not always better than explanation
- longform is not inherently doomed on公众号

The real issue is weak value delivery, weak structure, or weak share reason.

---

## Writing Rules Card

Use this as a default checklist:

1. Promise clearly in the title
2. Show payoff in the first screen
3. Write one point per paragraph
4. Give readers a forwarding reason
5. Use concrete language before abstraction
6. Use emotion with evidence, not as bait
7. End with one low-friction next step
