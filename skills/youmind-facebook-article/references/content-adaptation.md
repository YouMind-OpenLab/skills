# Facebook Content Adaptation Guide

## Platform Characteristics

- **Audience**: General, community-focused, broad demographics
- **Content format**: Plain text (no markdown rendering)
- **Algorithm**: Rewards engagement (comments, shares, reactions)
- **Reach**: Organic reach is limited; engagement-optimized content performs better

## Tone and Voice

- **Conversational**: Write like you're talking to a friend
- **Emotionally resonant**: Content that evokes emotion gets shared
- **Question-driven**: Ask questions to drive comments
- **Authentic**: Avoid corporate jargon; be real and relatable

## Content Structure

### Hook First Sentence
The first 1-2 lines must stop the scroll. Techniques:
- Bold claim or surprising statistic
- Relatable problem statement
- Provocative question
- Story opener ("Last week, something unexpected happened...")

### Short Paragraphs
- 1-3 sentences per paragraph maximum
- White space is your friend on mobile
- Break up walls of text aggressively

### Emoji Bullets
- Use emoji as visual bullets for lists
- Keep to 3-5 key points
- Each point should be scannable in 2 seconds

### Call to Action (CTA)
Every post should end with an engagement CTA:
- "What's your experience with this?"
- "Drop a comment if you agree"
- "Share this with someone who needs to hear it"
- "Tag a friend who..."

## Character Limits

| Type | Limit | Optimal |
|------|-------|---------|
| Post text | 63,206 chars | 40-80 words |
| Link post text | 63,206 chars | 1-2 sentences |
| Photo caption | 63,206 chars | 40-80 words |

**Optimal length for engagement**: 40-80 words (roughly 250-500 characters). Posts in this range get the highest engagement rates.

## Link Posts

- Placing a URL in the post body creates an automatic preview card
- The link preview pulls title, description, and image from the target page
- Keep accompanying text SHORT — let the preview card do the work
- Facebook de-prioritizes posts with links vs native content

## Image Guidelines

- **Square** (1:1): 1080x1080px — works well in feed
- **Landscape** (1.91:1): 1200x630px — optimal for link previews
- High contrast, readable text overlay if any
- Bright colors perform better in the feed
- Faces and people increase engagement

## Hashtag Strategy

- Facebook hashtags have LIMITED impact (unlike Instagram)
- Use 1-3 relevant hashtags maximum
- Place at the end of the post
- Use them for categorization, not discovery

## Formatting Tips

- Use line breaks liberally (Facebook collapses long posts with "See more")
- First 3 lines are the most important (visible before fold)
- Emojis increase engagement by ~25%
- ALL CAPS sparingly for emphasis (one word, not sentences)
