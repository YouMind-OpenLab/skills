# WordPress Content Adaptation Guide

- Treat WordPress content as a **post object**, not just a body: `title`, `slug`, `excerpt`, `featured image`, `status`, `categories`, and `tags` all matter.
- Use one clear title as the H1; do not repeat it in the body.
- Write an intentional excerpt instead of relying on the 55-word fallback when archive/search/share quality matters.
- Keep the opening highly informative: what the reader will learn, who it helps, and why it matters.
- Use H2/H3 structure, lists, tables, and code blocks that convert cleanly into block-editor-friendly HTML.
- Keep slugs short, stable, and aligned with the main query or article angle.
- Categories are for site structure; tags are for optional grouping. Do not blur them together.
- Plan at least one internal link when the site has related content; WordPress rewards strong site navigation.
- Featured images should be chosen for archive cards and social previews, not just for the article body.
- Default operational stance: `draft` first, use `future` for scheduled publishing when cadence matters, and rely on revisions for safe iteration.
