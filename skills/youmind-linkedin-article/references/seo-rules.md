# LinkedIn Algorithm Optimization

## Core Algorithm Signals

1. **Dwell time:** How long people read your post (longer = better)
2. **Engagement velocity:** Comments + reactions in the first 60 minutes
3. **Comment quality:** Longer comments signal higher-quality content
4. **Save/share ratio:** Saves indicate high-value content
5. **Connection relevance:** Content shown to connections first, then broader

## Optimization Checklist

### Hook (first 210 chars)
- [ ] Specific, not generic
- [ ] Creates curiosity gap (makes reader want to click "see more")
- [ ] No filler words
- [ ] Bold claim, number, or personal story

### Structure
- [ ] Short paragraphs (1–3 sentences)
- [ ] Visual line breaks between sections
- [ ] Scannable on mobile
- [ ] Key points in bold
- [ ] Total length: 1200–1500 chars for posts

### Engagement Design
- [ ] Ends with a specific, answerable question
- [ ] Question relates to reader's experience (not just opinion)
- [ ] Makes reader want to share their own story

### Timing
- **Best:** Tuesday–Thursday, 7–8 AM local time of target audience
- **Good:** Monday 8–10 AM, Wednesday 12–1 PM
- **Avoid:** Friday afternoon, weekends

### Anti-Suppression
- [ ] No external links in main post (put in first comment)
- [ ] No asking for likes/shares explicitly
- [ ] No tagging >5 people
- [ ] Image included (2–3x reach boost)
- [ ] No editing within first 10 minutes (resets algorithm)

## Post-Publish Actions

1. **Reply to every comment** within 2 hours (signals active discussion)
2. **Add the link** as a first comment (if you have one to share)
3. **Engage with others' posts** for 15 minutes before and after posting (reciprocity algorithm)
