# LinkedIn Content Adaptation Guide

## Character Limits

- **Post body**: 3,000 characters maximum
- **First fold ("see more")**: ~210 characters visible before truncation
- **Comment**: 1,250 characters
- **Article (long-form)**: 125,000 characters (separate feature, not covered here)

## Formatting Rules

LinkedIn does NOT support Markdown. Use Unicode formatting instead:

- **Bold**: Use Unicode Mathematical Bold characters (U+1D400 range)
- **Italic**: Use Unicode Mathematical Italic characters (U+1D434 range)
- **Bullets**: Use Unicode bullet character (U+2022)
- **Line breaks**: Use actual line breaks (LinkedIn preserves them)
- **Emojis**: Supported and recommended for visual breaks

## Content Structure

### Hook (Lines 1-2)
The first ~210 characters appear before "see more". This is critical:
- Start with a bold statement, surprising fact, or provocative question
- Create curiosity gap that compels clicking "see more"
- Do NOT start with "I'm excited to share..." or similar weak openers

### Body (Lines 3+)
- Short paragraphs: 1-3 sentences max
- Use white space generously (blank lines between paragraphs)
- Include bullet points for scannable content
- Add personal anecdotes or specific data points
- One idea per paragraph

### CTA (Last section)
- End with a question to drive comments
- Make it specific and answerable (not "what do you think?")
- Good: "What's one tool that changed your workflow this year?"
- Bad: "Agree? Disagree? Let me know!"

### Hashtags (Very end)
- 3-5 hashtags at the very end
- Mix broad (#AI) and niche (#MLOps) tags
- Check hashtag follower counts on LinkedIn

## Algorithm Optimization

1. **No external links in body** -- LinkedIn suppresses posts with links. Put links in the first comment instead.
2. **Encourage comments** -- Comments boost reach more than reactions.
3. **Post timing** -- Best times: Tuesday-Thursday, 8-10 AM local time.
4. **Engagement in first hour** -- Respond to every comment in the first 60 minutes.
5. **Dwell time** -- Longer posts that keep readers scrolling perform better.

## Link Strategy

- Extract all URLs from the post body
- Remove them from the main text
- Suggest posting them as the first comment
- This avoids LinkedIn's link suppression algorithm

## Image Guidelines

- Recommended size: 1200x627 pixels (1.91:1 ratio)
- Carousel/document posts often outperform single images
- Infographics and charts get high engagement
- Avoid stock photos -- authentic images perform better
