# LinkedIn Visual Design Guide

## Image Impact on Reach

LinkedIn posts with images get 2–3x more impressions than text-only posts.
Posts with native documents/carousels get even higher engagement.

## Image Types by Content

### For Posts
- **Single image:** Professional, relevant to topic
- **Infographic:** Data visualization, process diagrams
- **Quote card:** Pull quote from your post on branded background
- **Screenshot:** Real product/tool screenshots feel authentic
- **Carousel (PDF):** Multi-slide format, highest engagement

### For Articles
- **Cover image:** 1200x628px, represents the topic
- **Inline images:** Diagrams, charts, screenshots
- **Pull quotes:** Visually broken-out key insights

## Prompt Design for LinkedIn

Professional, clean aesthetic. LinkedIn audience expects polished visuals.

### Cover Images
- Clean, minimal design with subtle gradients
- Abstract or conceptual (not literal stock photo style)
- **Must include in prompt:** `no text, no letters, no words, professional, clean, minimal`
- Aspect ratio: 1200x628 (1.91:1) for article covers
- Color: Match brand palette or topic mood

### Inline Images
- Diagrams and flowcharts work best
- Data visualizations with clean design
- Screenshots of real tools/products
- **Avoid:** Generic stock photo style, overly colorful, busy compositions

## Color Psychology for LinkedIn

| Topic | Color Direction |
|-------|----------------|
| Technology/AI | Blue, cyan, dark backgrounds |
| Leadership | Navy, gold, warm whites |
| Growth/Success | Green, warm tones |
| Innovation | Purple, gradient, modern |
| Data/Analytics | Blue, grey, clean whites |
