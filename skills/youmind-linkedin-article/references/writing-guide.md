# LinkedIn Writing Craft Guide

> The gap between AI-generated LinkedIn content and posts that build real influence
> is not vocabulary — it's the ability to make professional insights feel personal.

---

## The Core Problem

AI writes LinkedIn posts that sound like corporate communications: correct, comprehensive, forgettable. LinkedIn users scroll past hundreds of "I'm excited to announce" and "Here are 5 tips for" posts daily.

Your job is to make them stop, think, and engage. The standard you're writing against is not "acceptable professional content" — it's the best creators on the platform who turn personal experiences into industry insights.

## The Hook — Everything Starts Here

LinkedIn truncates posts after ~210 characters with "...see more". Those first 2 lines are your entire pitch.

### Hook Patterns That Work

1. **Contrarian opener:** "Everyone says X. They're wrong. Here's why."
2. **Specific number:** "I interviewed 47 CTOs last month. One pattern shocked me."
3. **Personal vulnerability:** "I got fired from my dream job last year. Best thing that happened."
4. **Bold claim:** "The best engineers I've hired in 2026 don't have CS degrees."
5. **Pattern interrupt:** Start with a single word or phrase. Then expand.

### Hooks That FAIL

- "I'm excited to share..." (nobody cares about your excitement)
- "As a [title], I believe..." (leads with authority instead of value)
- "In today's fast-paced world..." (generic, skip-worthy)
- Questions without stakes ("What do you think about AI?")

## Voice Architecture

### Level 1: Professional Authenticity

- First person, confident but not arrogant
- Use "I" liberally — LinkedIn is personal
- Share real numbers: revenue, users, team size, time invested
- Admit failures alongside successes
- Use conversational transitions: "Here's the thing.", "But wait.", "The twist?"

### Level 2: Insight Density

Every paragraph must pass the "So what?" test:
- BAD: "AI is transforming the workplace" (everyone knows this)
- GOOD: "We replaced our 6-person content team with 2 people + Claude. Output went up 3x. Quality? The 2 people say it's better because they spend time editing instead of drafting."

### Level 3: Structure for Mobile

80% of LinkedIn is read on mobile. Format accordingly:
- Short paragraphs (1–3 sentences max)
- Line breaks between paragraphs (visual breathing room)
- No walls of text
- Use emoji sparingly as section markers (🔑, 📊, 💡) — max 3–4 per post
- Bold key phrases for scanners

### Level 4: Anti-AI Filter

Remove ALL of:
- "Leverage", "synergy", "stakeholder", "paradigm shift"
- "I'm thrilled/excited/delighted to..."
- "Let's dive in", "Without further ado"
- "In conclusion", "To sum up"
- Generic closings ("What are your thoughts?")
- Perfectly balanced paragraphs (vary length deliberately)

## Post vs Article Voice

### Post Voice
- Punchy, direct, opinionated
- One core idea, fully explored
- Ending: specific question or provocative statement
- Tone: smart friend giving career advice over coffee

### Article Voice  
- More structured, but still personal
- Multiple ideas connected by a narrative thread
- Data-heavy sections balanced with personal anecdotes
- Tone: respected industry expert writing a column

## Bilingual Notes / 双语备注

For Chinese-language LinkedIn content:
- LinkedIn 中文内容面向职场人士，但不要写成 OA 公文
- 避免空洞的职场鸡汤（"感恩遇见"、"砥砺前行"），写具体的经验和数据
- 可以中英混用技术术语（自然的方式，不是刻意的）
- 开头 hook 同样重要——前两行决定是否展开阅读
- 个人经历 + 行业洞察 = 最佳组合
- 适当使用数字和具体细节增强可信度
